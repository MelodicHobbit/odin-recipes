Philly Cheesesteak

Home

This Philly cheesesteak is made with sirloin sliced into strips and seasoned with a delicous blend of herbs and spices for a flavorsome sandwich.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried marjoram
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 pound of beef sirloin, cut into 2 inch strips
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, julienned
  • 3 ounces of swiss cheese, thinly sliced
  • 4 rolls, split lengthwise

How to Prepare

  1. Mix salt, pepper, paprika, chili powder, onion powder, garlic powder, thyme, marjoram, and basil together in a small bowl.
  2. Place your steak in a large bowl; sprinkle seasoning mixture over the top and stir to coat.
  3. Heat 1/2 of the oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the steak in batches if needed to avoid overcrowding the skillet; cook until browned and just cooked through. Transfer your cooked steak onto a plate.
  4. Heat the remaining oil in the skillet. Add onion and green pepper; cook and stir until tender and caramelized.
  5. Preheat your oven on the broiler setting. Divide the cooked beef between the bottoms of 4 rolls.
  6. Layer with onion and green pepper.
  7. Top with sliced cheese. Place on grease-proof paper.
  8. Broil in the preheated oven until the cheese melts.
  9. Cover with the tops of your rolls, serve and enjoy!
